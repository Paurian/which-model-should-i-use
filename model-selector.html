Survival Models Multiple entities, time-to-event A SaaS company models time-to-churn for each subscriber using Cox Proportional Hazards — identifying that low login frequency is the strongest predictor of early cancellation.
Clustering Modelstime-based Multiple entities, not time-to-event, grouping An e-commerce company clusters customers by their 12-month purchase cadence, discovering "Seasonal Splurgers", "Steady Loyalists", and "Dormant Browsers" — without predicting a specific target.
Panel Models Multiple entities, not time-to-event, not grouping Economists measure the effect of state-level minimum wage increases on employment rates across 50 states over 10 years — using fixed-effects panel regression to control for state-specific differences.
Clustering Modelscross-sectional No time component, no outcome variable A bank segments current customers by demographics and product holdings (balance, loan status, age) to target marketing — no target variable, just grouping.
Causal Influence Modelscross-sectional No time component, has outcome, causal A retailer runs a promotional discount in select stores and uses difference-in-differences to determine whether the promotion caused a revenue lift — not predicting future values, establishing causation.
